NYC short-term rental regulations face rocky rollout

Genna Contino

NEW YORK - New regulations were implemented in September in effort to crack down on illegal short-term rentals in New York City. But as the hosts of legal short-term rentals are registering their units with the city, the majority of them are sitting in pending status.

Of the more than 1,300 applications, 1,207 are sitting in a pending status or are missing information for the application. Twenty four were denied.

In the past two months, more than 70% of Airbnb listings have been taken down, leaving tourists and business travelers with less options for lodging.

The city is sacrificing the potential loss for tourists as a win for New Yorkers struggling to find housing.

“You are, in a way, constraining the supply of housing for both rental and also ownership,” said Urban Institute research associate Jorge González-Hermoso. “And with a constrained supply, and especially in cities like New York which have very high demand for housing, you are increasing the prices of this housing.”
